As part of the Sustainability team at Henning Larsen Architects, I was involved in formulating the Comfort strategy that guided the volumetric massing of ILMI Museum and generating the design for the “Cloud” that is multi-functional in nature. Sustainability is not a choice - it is a premise. ILMI, a significant part of the larger MiSK City,has the potential to become a catalyst for creating life and adding value to the entire MISK masterplan. I do not see the building as an isolated object, but believe it can generate critical impact on how MISK and the city life within, are shaped. Throughout the design development, I was focussed to implement and operationalize knowledge, in order to guide the team to environmentally sound decisions and solutions. The focus has been on robust and integrated solutions that create long-term use-value. Continous analytical evaluation of different components that holisticaly form the museum, based primarily on improving the microclimate, were performed and the design was developed in line with the vision of the MiSK City.
The Cloud, essentially the roof of the museum, is inspired by science, art, and technology. It is an icon for ILMI that hovers above the plateau. Below the Cloud, an oasis thrives in the protection of the shadow. The ILMI Oasis, shaped by the various sunken courtyards and museum galleries, is also a cultural oasis where residents and visitors alike meet, socialize, and exchange ideas. The cloud has three layers. The upper layer of the cloud is facing the sun and is equipped with photovoltaic panels, providing a sustainable energy source for the museum. The bottom of the cloud, the surface of the cloud that defines its shape, it is what you see from afar and what is closest to the landscapes underneath. In this zone the cloud is fitted with elements that regulate the microclimate, providing shade and guiding the wind. Between these upper and the lower layers is the fit-out zone. In this part of the cloud, only the imagination or advances in art, science and technolgy limits the possibilities.
